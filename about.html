Background

The Young Lawyers In Tech (#weLIT) is a network of students, early-career researchers, and practitioners interested in technology and Innovation law. #weLIT coordinates a wide range of programs to support the professional development and intellectual growth of members over the course of their lawtech career.

Our Goals
  1. To foresee legal dilemmas presented by emerging technologies and innovate solutions for them.
  2. To train Mentor and expose young lawyers to key practice areas of technology and Innovation law.
  3. To create new and exciting events surrounding legal tech and bring new opportunities to law students and young lawyers to help them kickstart their legal tech journey.
Our Vision

To create a global network of young law tech enthusiasts across all educational institutions and provide information, education, peer connections, and career support to the community to help them kickstart their law tech journey.

Focus Areas

Join our community of young LawTech enthusiasts and match your goals to our programs, explore your options and map out your path to a successful Lawtech career in the following areas.

Intellectual Property Law

Intellectual Property Law deals with laws to protect and enforce rights of the creators and owners of inventions, writing, music, designs and other works, known as the "intellectual property." We engage and train our members on illustrative issues surrounding various Intellectual Property rights, how they are created and how to protect them.

Digital Rights and Policy

This society empowers young lawyers to identify illustrative issues on challenges to digital rights on the continent, and suggests some actions that should be undertaken to address them. These include advocacy against: regressive online content regulation and taxation, Network Disruptions and internet shutdowns, Misuse of sensitive personal data.

Data Protection & Privacy

There has been a burning need and concern to ensure proper regulation of the processing of personal data through adequate protection of the rights of data subjects as well as clear obligations of data controllers and processors. There is a great need for data protection and privacy rights practitioners thus we aim to create a smooth transition for early-career practitioners into such areas of practice.

Cybercrimes

Criminal activities over internet have become a global concern. They incude: Unauthorised access, interference and interception, Cyber espionage, False publications, Child pornography, Computer forgery and fraud, Cyber harassment, Cybersquatting, Identity theft and impersonation among others. There is a need to breed a generation of smart, innovative and vigilant lawyers who are well trained and equipped to combat cybercrimes both locally and globally.

Internet Governance

Internet governance was defined is the complementary development and application by governments, the private sector, civil society and the technical community, in their respective roles, of shared principles, norms, rules, decision-making procedures, and activities that shape the evolution and use of the Internet. We aspire to create opportunities for our members to contribute to conversations around the evolution and regulation of the internet.

Contemporary Technologies

Technology is rapidly evolving at a pace never witnessed before. Whereas legal frameworks are isntrumental in protecting consumers, laws might be too slow to ctach up thus leading to regulatory gaps which end up being blamed for stalling progress. For instance, even before Kenya has conclusivelly set up comprehensive laws on cryptocurrencies, blockchain technology has already introduced NFTs, Virtual Real estate on meta-verse etc.
